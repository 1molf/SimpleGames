Four in a Row
How to Play Four in a Row
Objective: The objective of Four in a Row is to be the first player to connect four of your colored discs in a row, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Setup: Four in a Row is played on a vertical grid board with six rows and seven columns. Each player has a set of colored discs, usually one color is red and the other is yellow.

Turns: Players take turns dropping one of their colored discs into any of the empty slots on the top of the board.

Winning: The game ends when one player successfully connects four of their colored discs in a row. This can be horizontally, vertically, or diagonally. The game also ends in a draw if the entire board is filled with discs and no player has won.

Strategy: To win, players must strategically place their discs to block their opponent from connecting four in a row while simultaneously trying to create their own winning combination.

Ending the Game: Once a player wins or the game ends in a draw, players can start a new game by resetting the board and taking turns again.

Have Fun: Four in a Row is a classic and simple game that requires strategic thinking and planning. Enjoy the challenge of outsmarting your opponent and connecting four in a row!

Remember, the game is easy to learn but can be challenging to master. So, keep practicing and have fun playing Four in a Row!
